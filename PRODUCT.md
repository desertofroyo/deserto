# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Deserto serves Tucson, AZ café-goers and the broader "modern urban foodie"
audience the brand book names, across three primary targets:

- **The Urban Foodie** — seeks novelty, quality, and a place worth posting; wants
  the *health-meets-indulgence* story and the ritual of treating themselves.
- **The Busy Parent** — wants a quick, friendly, family-pleasing stop; values
  clarity (what's vegan/GF), speed of ordering, and warmth.
- **The Fitness Enthusiast** — drawn by the "good-for-you" yogur side (vegan, GF,
  lower-cal options) without feeling lectured at.

Context of use is split across two surfaces that share one identity: a
**marketing website** (discover the brand, browse the menu, find the café) and a
**mobile ordering app + online order page** (build a froyo, order pickup/delivery,
earn rewards). This document's default register is **brand** (the marketing
surface leads); app/order tasks override to `product` per task.

## Product Purpose

Deserto — "dessert" + *desierto* (desert), one invented name in two languages —
is a self-serve frozen-yogurt & specialty café. The digital product exists to
make the brand's promise tangible: **health and indulgence at the same table,
your way** (*mi gusto, mi estilo*). Success is a site and app that feel as warm,
vibrant, and confident as the café itself — appetite-forward, photography-led,
and unmistakably Deserto — while letting guests browse the real Toast menu and
order without friction.

## Brand Personality

Three words, straight from the brand book: **Cálida (warm), Innovadora
(innovative), Creativa (creative)**. The voice is a knowledgeable friend who's
genuinely excited about good food and good coffee — sophisticated but
accessible, never clinical. Spanish-first for emotional/brand moments (`Mi gusto,
mi estilo`, `Primavera / Verano / Otoño`), English for functional UI; the two sit
comfortably together. Address the guest as **you**; the brand is **we / Deserto**.

- **Say:** savor · fresh · your way · indulge · wholesome · crafted · seasonal ·
  treat yourself.
- **Don't say:** cheap · diet · guilt-free · low-fat (as a primary hook) ·
  corporate jargon · hype-y exclamation spam.
- **No emoji** in product or marketing copy — expression comes from the winking
  isotype, color, and photography.

## Anti-references

Pulled from the 2024 Brand Book (the source of truth) and the design system's
own visual-foundations rules. Deserto must NOT look like:

- **A generic restaurant template** — bland sticky-nav + split-hero + uniform
  card-grid with no point of view. The brand earns its identity with color
  blocking, big Anisette display type, the arch motif, and real photography.
- **AI-slop / tech aesthetics** — blue or bluish-purple gradients, glassmorphism,
  neon accents, emoji cards, or cards with a colored left-border stripe. These
  are explicitly off-brand.
- **The cool/pastel mauve drift** — the earlier lavender-walls / sage / dusty-rose
  recalibration that was reverted in favor of the warm brand-book nine. Backgrounds
  are warm (peach-cream, white, bold brand blocks), never cool pastel.
- **Corporate/minimalist white** — stark all-white, grey-on-grey SaaS minimalism
  with cold grey/black shadows and sharp corners. Too cold for a warm froyo café.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth is non-negotiable.** Every neutral leans espresso/peach-cream; shadows
   are warm brown, never grey or black. The page should feel like warm daylight.
2. **Color confidently, in blocks.** Use the nine brand colors as bold full-bleed
   blocks (wine, orange, olive, lime), the way the brand book bands color — not as
   timid tints. Wine grounds, orange is the rare energetic pop.
3. **Photography does the selling.** Lead hero and feature moments with real,
   appetite-forward café photography; never hand-drawn filler or stock clichés.
4. **Health meets indulgence — show both.** Surface vegan/GF/cal signals plainly
   without ever making "diet" the hook. Indulgence is the feeling; wholesome is
   the reassurance.
5. **Playful, but never tacky.** Soft pill shapes, the winking isotype, quick
   ease-out motion. Delight comes from craft and color, not from bouncy gimmicks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**: AA contrast on text and meaningful UI, a visible `2px`
orange focus ring at `2px` offset, and full `prefers-reduced-motion` support
(already wired — all animation is disabled under reduced motion). Honor bilingual
users (Spanish/English) and keep tap targets comfortable on the mobile app. Color
is never the sole carrier of meaning (tags also use text labels).
